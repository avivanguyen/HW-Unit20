BROOMmates

Have you ever had a dispute about chores with your friends, family, or significant other that you live with? About who should be washing dishes, throwing the trash away, vacuuming, etc. Experienced how quickly a relationship can sour after confronting them about not doing their share to keep the house clean? This app would help schedule chores amongst those cohabitating with you to reduce the disputes and confrontations that can strain relationships.Â After all, a clean home is a happy home.

  • Collaboration with three other UX designers and individually.
  • Tools: Figma, Miro, Trello, and Zoom.
  • Time Frame: Three weeks.

    • The Problem:

    When living with cohabitants, dividing up chores can be frustrating due to a lack of communication or scheduling.

    The Solution:

    BROOMmates will be an app that is designed to help housemates coordinate and schedule household chores. The app will provide an option for users to send friendly reminders of unfinished tasks that need to be completed.

    • Improved household organization
    • Eliminate frustration by easily creating tasks and sending reminderes
    • Easy to view to-do list

    User Interview

    For our interviews, we each interviewed four people (a total of 16 interviews) that are at least 20 years old or older, living with roommates or their significant other.

    The objectives of the interview were:

  • To understand the mindset of individuals around chores.
  • To understand the user's struggles (if any) in scheduling their chores.
  • To understand ho to help the user complete household chores.

    • From our interviews, we organized the interviewee's answers and categorized them within an Affinity Diagram. Keeping in mind the insights that the interviewees provided helped us focus on features for our app, one of the main features mentioned would be a scheduling platform. Adults have many other obligations to do throughout their day and there are times that things slip from our minds. Coming home from a hard day's work drains the energy already. Hoping that our direction for the app can help users feel more at ease with cleaning chores.

    Key Findings

    First Prototype Testing

    With my team, we started sketching ideas on Figma based on the user flow for a low-fidelity wireframe. However, after creating our prototype individually, and performing our first user testing, we noticed that we each had our own ideas for the layout of the "Home" page. It was interesting to see that we had similar designs for the "Task Dashboard" and the "Team Hub" but completely had different designs for the "Home".

    One takeaway was that not everyone would have the same thought process and you can design something in various ways even if you briefly discussed the elements of the design.

    User Testing Tasks:
